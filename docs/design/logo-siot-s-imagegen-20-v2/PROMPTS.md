## Master prompt — apply verbatim to every concept

```text
Use case: logo-brand
Asset type: mobile app icon symbol exploration for SingSong
Input image: Image 1 is a rough composition reference only, not an edit target.

Primary request: Design ONE original logo mark whose construction material is the Korean consonant `ㅅ` (siot). Use exactly TWO complete siot glyph modules to build ONE overall capital Latin `S` flow. This is not two ordinary carets stacked vertically: the upper siot must pull the eye toward the left-middle, the lower siot must return it toward the right-bottom, and their overlap or handoff must make the total silhouette unmistakably S-shaped.

Hangul legibility invariant: each module must still read to a Korean viewer as `ㅅ` — a visible upper apex where two strokes meet, plus two descending arms. Preserve both complete apexes. Mild bending, unequal arm length, overlap, and rotation are allowed; do not deform either module into a plain V, arrow, greater-than/less-than sign, zigzag, Latin M, lightning bolt, or generic ribbon.

Style/medium: exceptionally clean vector-like logo design, flat solid shapes, precise geometry, strong silhouette, professional identity-system quality, no texture.
Composition/framing: one centered symbol only, large generous margin, square 1:1 canvas, optically balanced for an app icon, readable at 24 px.
Color palette: warm off-white background #FAF7F0; only near-black #15131A and vivid coral pink #FF3D6E for the mark. The two complete siot modules may use one color each.
Constraints: no wordmark, no Latin or Korean text outside the symbol, no labels, no numbers, no border, no app-device mockup, no hands, no screenshot UI, no staff lines, no gradients, no shadows, no 3D, no watermark, no music note, no microphone, no headphones, no play button, no heart. Do not add a third glyph. Deliver a single isolated logo concept.
```

## Twenty controlled concept modifiers

Append exactly one modifier below to the master prompt for each generation.

1. **Connected handoff** — upper coral siot shifted right and lower black siot shifted left; one inner arm from each meets at the waist to create a clean continuous S centerline while both apexes remain visible.
2. **Open waist** — two complete siots nearly touch but leave one deliberate narrow negative-space gap at the S waist; rounded terminals, no overlap.
3. **Woven crossing** — the inner arms cross once at the S waist with a clean over-under weave; upper coral passes over lower black; no extra strands.
4. **Shared junction** — one compact diamond-like junction is shared by the two inner arms, but the upper and lower siot apexes remain separate and obvious.
5. **Long-short rhythm** — each siot has one long arm following the outer S contour and one short arm protecting Hangul legibility; sharply controlled proportions.
6. **Soft ribbon geometry** — gently bowed strokes with pointed siot apexes and rounded terminals; the S rhythm is fluid but the mark stays flat and geometric.
7. **Hard angular geometry** — straight strokes, crisp mitred corners, diagonal cuts at terminals; unmistakable `ㅅ` modules and a decisive S silhouette.
8. **Counter-tilt** — upper siot rotated no more than 18 degrees counter-clockwise and lower siot no more than 18 degrees clockwise, creating the sketch's alternating pull without becoming `<` or `>`.
9. **Scale contrast** — upper siot is about 20 percent larger than lower siot; their inner arms align into one S spine; both glyphs remain complete.
10. **Reverse scale contrast** — lower siot is about 20 percent larger than upper siot; balanced asymmetric app-icon silhouette.
11. **Nested waist** — the tip of one upper arm tucks into the open interior of the lower siot without hiding its apex; clear two-glyph construction and S motion.
12. **Interlocking hooks** — only the inner terminals curve into subtle opposing hooks that lock at the waist; no loops and no chain-link appearance.
13. **Parallel lane** — small, even negative-space channel between overlapping inner arms makes the S spine; outer arms remain independent and clearly form the two siots.
14. **Cut-gap S** — two precisely placed cuts in the long inner arms create a rhythmic S path while each siot still has a connected apex and two readable arms.
15. **Capsule monoline** — uniform very bold monoline with fully rounded caps, compact friendly proportions, exact two-siot S construction.
16. **Tapered calligraphic** — controlled modest taper from apex to terminals, no brush texture; sophisticated but still geometric and scalable.
17. **Pinched waist** — generous upper and lower bowls made from extended outer arms, narrowing tightly at the meeting waist; not an hourglass or number 8.
18. **Offset overlap** — upper module floats slightly forward/right and lower module back/left with a single small overlap; preserve equal visual weight and both apexes.
19. **Negative-space S** — two chunky complete siot shapes interlock so the off-white gap between them traces a clean capital S; the positive shapes must still read as two `ㅅ` glyphs.
20. **Essential minimum** — reduce the idea to the fewest possible bold strokes while still showing exactly two complete siot apexes, four descending arms in total, and one unmistakable overall S flow.
